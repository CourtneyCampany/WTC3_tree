WTCIII-Figures

Court Campany

June 23, 2015

Design

  • WTCIII was a warming experiment with ambient and +3°C warming (n=6). A drought manipulation was added in the last few months of the experiment but is not included here.
  • Sun leaves were measured in the upper canopy at their current PPFD. Shade leaves were measured in the lower/middle canopy, first at their current PPFD and then again at sun leaf PPFD as a simulated sunfleck.
  • New fully expanded leaves were measured. Shade leaves were sampled into the middle canopy as the experiment progressed to avoid issues with leaf age.
  • Leaf physiological measurements were taken at their current temperature (ambient, +3°C). Thus, data below represent a large range of temperatures from 18-35°C.

Figure 1. Local light environment for sun and shade leaves for each gas exchange campaign. Means and standard errors represent 1m integrated PPFD (n=5), measured with a ceptometer, at the canopy height of each selected leaf.

Figure2. Predicted photosynthetic CO2 response (ACi) curves for sun and shade leaves at elevated and ambient temperature treatments. ACi curves were developed at 25°C and at saturating light (1800 \(\mu\)mols m-1 s-1). ACi curves were taken once on well watered trees before the initiation of the drought treatment.

Figure 3. Response of instantaneous water use efficiency (ITE) to leaf vapor pressure deficit (VPD) (a) and response of leaf photosynthesis rates to stomatal conductance (b) for sun and shade leaves at elevated and ambient temperature treatments. For (a) lines represent predictions from the optimal ITE model with a g1 value for each leaf type and treatment. For (b) dashed lines represent 95% confidence intervals from a general additive model with k=5.

Figure 4. The relationship between leaf \(\delta\)13C and nitrogenarea for sun and shade leaves at elevated and ambient temperature treatments.

Figure 5. Response of mesophyll conductance to leaf temperature (a) and the response of leaf photosynthesis to mesophyll conductance (b) for sun and shade leaves at elevated and ambient temperature treatments. The mean difference in mesophyll conductance (c) and photosynthesis (d) between sun and shade leaves with standard errors. For (a) and (b) lines represent 95% confidence intervals generated with bootstrapping methods (n=999) from mixed effect linear model equations with chamber as a random effect.

Figure 6. Response of photosynthesis to mesophyll conductance following a maximum sunfleck response of shade leaves (a) and overall mean values of mesophyll conductance (c) and photosynthesis (d) to sun flecks in shade leaves with standard errors. The sunfleck was simulated by increasing the PPFD to the level of the sun leaf in the same chamber. The response of leaf physiology to sun flecks was recorded once both photosynthesis and stomatal conductance were stable in the gas exchange leaf cuvette (ca. 25min).
